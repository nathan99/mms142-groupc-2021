What is AR & VR?

Posted by Arvic Cruz on December 12, 2021

Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR), along with Mixed Reality (MR) which tries to integrate real and virtual worlds, are part of what is being called the eXtended Reality (XR).

"eXtended Reality (XR) is a ‘catch-all’ term for technologies that enhance or replace our view of the world. This is often through overlaying or immersing computer text and graphics into real-world and virtual environments, or even a combination of both." -Hooker, 2021.

Furthermore, these comprise the Immersive Technologies. Immersive Technologies provide users prominent involvement in the integration between the digital simulation of reality and the physical world. However, AR and VR are the two leading forms of Immersive Technologies.

According to Hayes (December, 2020), Augmented reality (AR) is a simulation of the physical world with the help of visual, audio, or other sensory technologies. In the same manner, it "blends computer-generated information onto the user’s real environment." (Vista Equity, 2021). It can be found in phones with AR support. An example of an augmented reality is Pokemon Go by Nintendo where the smartphone’s camera captures the area while reflecting it with the creature on the screen.

On the other hand, according to Lowood (May, 2021), Virtual reality (VR) makes the interaction between computer models and the user possible with the help of a visual equipment or accessory. Hence, according to Vista Equity (2021), it "uses computer-generated information to provide a full sense of immersion."
